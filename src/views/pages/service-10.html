International
Travel Certificate

International travel with your pet usually requires a check-up by a USDA-certified vet and different rules based on the country you're going to, plus getting a health certificate approved by a vet. Our traveling vets are USDA-certified, have the power to issue a USDA-certified pet travel certificate, and will come to you!

Request appointment

What is an International Travel Certificate?

An international travel certificate is paperwork that shows your pet's health requirements like shots, tests, and exams for traveling between the U.S. and other countries. You need to follow strict rules to cross borders. We make sure your pet follows the rules of the country you're going to.

What to Expect with an International Travel Certificate

You need an appointment so that one of our vets can check your pet and confirm your pet's health status. We will look at your pet's shot records and order any required lab tests you've agreed to, like rabies titers, blood tests, and poop or heartworm exams.

Many countries need treatment for bugs inside/outside your pet within a certain time before travel. We finish all necessary paperwork and government forms needed to get an approved international health certificate from the USDA APHIS agency. This must go with your pet.

How an International Travel Certificate Benefits You and Your Pet

Figuring out international pet travel rules is hard. We handle those details so you can focus on other trip stuff. Our experience with the latest foreign country requirements helps avoid delays or rejections. Valid certificates prevent cargo quarantines.

Careful vet check-up and testing reduce the chance of getting sick while traveling. Most importantly, proper rules keep your pet safe and comfy arriving at their international place.

Let Vets ASAP handle the hassle of international pet travel logistics smoothly so your furry buddy can explore the world with you. Contact us today!
