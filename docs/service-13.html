What is a Vet Neurology Exam?

A vet neurology exam is when we check how your pet's brain and nerves work. It helps us find out if your pet has any problems with their nervous system or behavior that could mean they have a brain or nerve disease.

What to Expect During a Vet Neurology Exam

You need an appointment so that one of our vets can check your pet and confirm your pet's health status. We will look at your pet's health history and the changes you've seen. We'll do a detailed neurology test, including reflex, feeling, balance, and nerve tests, as well as checking eyes, ears, and general brain function.

If needed, we may suggest more tests like MRI, CT scan, spinal fluid test, electric test, or gene test to fully find out what's wrong with your pet's brain or nerves and rule out other problems. With an accurate diagnosis, we can give you targeted treatment suggestions and steps.

How a Vet Neurology Exam Benefits Your Pet

Finding out the exact neurology problem through a skilled exam and tests is key for the right treatment to ease symptoms, slow down progression, and support the best possible quality of life. We help you figure out if problems are manageable or need more care.

Early diagnosis of many brain and nerve problems leads to better results. Your pet’s comfort and movement improve as we work to fix their neurology health and stability. FAQs

What common problems can your neurologists diagnose/treat?

Some common neurology problems we diagnose and manage include disc disease, seizure problems like epilepsy, old age balance problems, brain problems like abiotrophy, spine problems like embolism, nerve problems like neuropathy, brain tumors or other brain problems, and many other nerve and muscle problems.

Will I have to take imaging at a different place?

No, for your convenience, we have the most advanced on-site MRI, CT, and x-ray machines right here in our vet hospital so all imaging and tests can happen smoothly in one place. Avoiding going somewhere else makes the process easier for pets and owners alike.

What is the typical fee for a neurology consultation?

Our exam fees for an initial neurology check-up and chat start at around $xx, depending on how bad the problem is. Any extra imaging like MRI scans or spinal fluid tests would be quoted separately in addition to the base consultation fee. Please ask us for specific estimates.

Do you provide emergency neurology care?

Of course! We have board-certified vet neurology experts available 24/7 for urgent neuro chats in crisis situations where quick help is needed. If your pet experiences sudden mobility loss, seizure activity, or other major neurological symptoms, don't hesitate to contact us anytime - day or night.

Explore Our Services

Orthopedic Examination

As part of your pet's Wellness Exam, your Vets ASAP veterinarian will check your pet's orthopedic and neurological health. We can check how your pet's bones and joints work in different ways. Pets often hide their problems very well, so it is important for you to tell us about any changes you've noticed at your place.

Pet Pain Management

Whether your pet is having short-term pain or long-term aches and pains as they age or dealing with a chronic condition, our traveling vets will visit you and help your pet feel better.

Dermatologic Examination

During a Wellness or Sick Exam, our traveling vets can talk about how food or environmental allergies affect your pet and what to do for your pet. Pet allergy testing, diet tests, and referrals to a vet allergy expert might be helpful.

Electrocardiogram (ECG)

An important diagnostic tool to assess your pet’s health, an ECG measures the electrical activity of the heart and can spot any irregular or abnormal heartbeats. Depending on location, our vets can perform dog and cat ECGs in your home or the Vets ASAP van.

Microchipping

At-home cat and dog microchipping is an easy, stress-free, and long-term way to track down your lost pet. A pet microchip can work for up to 25 years and stay in place permanently, unlike other ID methods like a tag or a collar.

Veterinary Diagnostic Imaging

Vet X-ray is used to find out what's wrong with your pet. X-rays may be recommended when your vet needs a clear picture of what's inside your pet. Our care teams can do X-rays at your home or in our Vets ASAP van, wherever you are.
