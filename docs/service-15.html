What is Pet Pain Management?

Pet pain management refers to professional vet services focused on preventing, accurately diagnosing, and medically relieving acute or chronic pain in dogs, cats, and other animals. We use a combination of analgesics and anesthetic medications, physical therapy modalities, integrative care therapies, and advanced procedures to fully control and eliminate sources of pain or discomfort when possible.

What to Expect with Pet Pain Management

At your appointment, we assess pain levels and impacts through examination and feedback on your pet’s condition. Diagnostic tests may be recommended to determine pain origins. Considering risks and benefits, we devise a pain management plan that could incorporate NSAIDs, opioid medications, nutraceuticals, therapeutic laser therapy, acupuncture, rehabilitation exercises, dietary adjustments, and more. Home care instructions will be provided, along with follow-ups to monitor response. We work closely with your other vet specialists to optimize pain control.

How Pet Pain Management Benefits Your Animal

Thorough assessment allows tailored analgesic regimens to specifically target affected areas for substantive relief. Management prevents acute pain from becoming chronic through expedited healing and minimizes ongoing discomfort from chronic conditions. Your pet's mobility, activity tolerance, appetite, behaviors, and quality of life all improve as pain decreases.

Close monitoring safeguards against side effects. Pain control restores comfort and function for the best possible well-being. Relieve your pet’s suffering - contact Vets ASAP today to create a tailored vet pain management plan.

FAQs

What signs indicate my pet is in pain?

Changes in normal behavior patterns or activity levels, alterations in typical eating/drinking and sleeping habits, increased vocalizing/panting, unprovoked aggression, observable limping, stiffness or difficulty moving, constantly licking specific body areas, inappropriate elimination, lack of grooming, and other variations from baseline can all be subtle or overt signals your pet may be in pain and should be evaluated.

What options do you offer beyond medication?

Some excellent therapeutic options we offer alongside or instead of medications include vet therapeutic laser treatments to reduce inflammation and ease joint/muscle pain, vet acupuncture focused on proven pain pressure points, joint supplements like glucosamine to support mobility, custom orthopedic braces/supports to stabilize weak joints, and tailored physical rehabilitation programs incorporating massage, stretches, therapeutic exercises, hydrotherapy and more to rebuild strength and conditioning.

How much does pain management cost?

We offer free phone consultations to discuss your pet's needs and budget considerations. We customize treatment to meet each pet's unique requirements at various budget levels. Please inquire for specific estimates - controlling pain is a priority regardless of the financial situation.

Can you provide at-home hospice pain relief?

Absolutely. When a pet transitions to hospice care for terminal illnesses, we work closely with your family to create customized in-home plans that ensure your pet remains as comfortable, pain-free, and content as possible while surrounded by your love and care in their final days. Please let us know if you need assistance with planning a peaceful goodbye.

Explore Our Services

Neurological Examination

During a comprehensive in-home Wellness Exam or Sick Visit, our traveling vets can check your pet’s signs and brain function along with a complete nose-to-tail check-up of your pet’s wellness.

Orthopedic Examination

As part of your pet's Wellness Exam, your Vets ASAP veterinarian will check your pet's orthopedic and neurological health. We can check how your pet's bones and joints work in different ways. Pets often hide their problems very well, so it is important for you to tell us about any changes you've noticed at your place.

Dermatologic Examination

During a Wellness or Sick Exam, our traveling vets can talk about how food or environmental allergies affect your pet and what to do for your pet. Pet allergy testing, diet tests, and referrals to a vet allergy expert might be helpful.

Electrocardiogram (ECG)

An important diagnostic tool to assess your pet’s health, an ECG measures the electrical activity of the heart and can spot any irregular or abnormal heartbeats. Depending on location, our vets can perform dog and cat ECGs in your home or the Vets ASAP van.

Microchipping

At-home cat and dog microchipping is an easy, stress-free, and long-term way to track down your lost pet. A pet microchip can work for up to 25 years and stay in place permanently, unlike other ID methods like a tag or a collar.

Veterinary Diagnostic Imaging

Vet X-ray is used to find out what's wrong with your pet. X-rays may be recommended when your vet needs a clear picture of what's inside your pet. Our care teams can do X-rays at your home or in our Vets ASAP van, wherever you are.
