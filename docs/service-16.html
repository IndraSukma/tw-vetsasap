What is a Dermatology Exam?

A dermatology exam is when we check your petâ€™s skin, coat, ears, and nails for any problems and find out what's causing them. Skin problems are very common in pets and can be a sign of other health issues.

What to Expect During a Dermatology Exam

You need an appointment so that one of our vets can check your pet and confirm your pet's health status. We will look at your pet's skin history, including when/where problems happen and any changes. Your pet will get a detailed head-to-tail check-up to look at all parts of the skin, coat, and where skin meets other tissues.

We may do skin scrapings, tape preps, or fungal cultures to find parasites or germs. Bloodwork, allergy testing, or skin biopsies could be suggested. Based on exam results, we will figure out the problem and make a tailored treatment plan for relief and recovery.

How a Dermatology Exam Benefits Your Pet

Detailed dermatology testing helps us find the main and secondary causes of skin problems for targeted treatment. Symptoms can be a sign of bigger issues like metabolic disorders, infections, hormonal imbalances, or parasite infestations.

Early treatment leads to better results and less discomfort. We make a personalized treatment plan for your pet that includes medicines, bathing tips, food advice, and how to avoid allergens. Skin health gets better from the inside out!

FAQs

What are common skin problems you can diagnose/treat?

Some common skin problems we diagnose and provide personalized treatment plans for include allergies from food or environment or fleas, bacterial or yeast skin infections, bugs inside or outside your pet, autoimmune skin disorders, endocrine diseases, immune-mediated dermatitis, pattern baldness or hair loss problems, color changes like vitiligo, weird wounds or growths that need biopsy, and many other skin problems that affect the skin, coat, ears, or nails.

Should I give my pet a bath before the appointment?

No, please do not wash your pet for at least 3 days before their dermatology visit. We need to see and check their skin, fur, and any problems in their natural state without any recent shampoo or topical stuff.A clean coat can hide key details we need for proper diagnosis.

What is the typical fee for a dermatology consultation?

Our regular exam fees for an initial vet dermatology consultation depend on how bad the symptoms are. Any extra tests like skin scrapings or cultures, allergy testing bloodwork or skin biopsies would cost more. Please ask us for an estimate of how much dermatology care will cost.

Do you provide follow-up care?

Of course! We never want a pet suffering from long-term skin problems like allergies or repeated infections to feel left out. We work closely with you for the long run to keep improving your petâ€™s comfort and skin health through ongoing chats tests checking medicine adjustment avoiding allergens food management or other things as needed. Our team becomes your partner in keeping their best possible skin and fur condition over time.
